# Intranet

The Intranet is Collabase's internal communication layer. It surfaces blog posts, announcements, and company-wide updates in a single, chronological feed — so important information reaches the right people without competing with real-time chat.

Chat is effective for immediate conversation. It is poor at preserving information that needs to last longer than a day. When a policy changes, a product ships, or leadership has an announcement to make, that content needs its own space — findable, authored, and visible to everyone who should see it.

The Intranet gives structured communication a dedicated home inside the platform your team already uses.

## The feed

The Intranet feed is accessible from the **main navigation**, outside of any individual Space. It shows blog posts from all Spaces you have access to, ordered by publish date — newest first.

You do not need to navigate to individual Spaces to find content. Everything published to the Intranet surfaces here automatically, filtered to what you are permitted to see.

## Announcements

Announcements are pinned blog posts that stay prominently visible at the top of the feed. Use them for time-sensitive company-wide communication — new policies, major releases, or anything the whole organisation needs to notice.

To pin a post, open its context menu and select **Pin to Intranet**. To unpin it, open the same menu and select **Unpin from Intranet**. Archived posts are removed from the feed entirely but remain accessible in the Space where they were created.

## Permission model

The Intranet feed respects Space visibility — you see posts from Spaces you have access to, and nothing else.

| Space visibility | Who sees the posts in the feed |
|---|---|
| `PRIVATE` | Only members of that Space |
| `INTERNAL` | All logged-in users across the organisation |

This means you can publish department-specific updates from a private Space and company-wide announcements from an internal Space — and each user's feed is automatically filtered to what they should see. No manual audience selection required.

<Note>
  There is no separate permission model for the Intranet. Access is determined entirely by Space membership and Space visibility settings.
</Note>
