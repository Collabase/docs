Collabase gives you full control over who can access your instance, what they can do, and how they are provisioned. All user and group management lives under **Admin → Users** and **Admin → Groups**.

## Global roles

Every user in Collabase has a single global role that determines their baseline access to the platform.

| Role | What they can do |
|---|---|
| **Admin** | Full access to all admin settings, all Spaces, and all system configuration. Admins can manage users, groups, identity providers, and instance settings. |
| **User** | Can create and edit content in any Space they are explicitly granted access to. Cannot access Admin settings. |

<Info>
  Global roles set the ceiling for what a user can do. Space-level roles further restrict access within a specific Space. See [Permissions](/concepts/permissions) for the full model.
</Info>

## Managing users

Navigate to **Admin → Users** to see all users in your instance. The users table shows each user's name, email, global role, creation date, last login, and directory source (Local, LDAP, SCIM, OAuth, etc.).

### Adding a user

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the add user dialog">
    Click **Add New User** in the top-right corner of **Admin → Users**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the user's details">
    Fill in the user's full name and email address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a temporary password">
    Enter a temporary password for the account (minimum 8 characters). The user is required to change their password on first login.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign a global role">
    Choose **User** or **Admin**. You can change this at any time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create User">
    The account is created immediately and appears in the users table. Share the credentials securely with the new user.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Changing a user's role

In the users table, open the user's row actions and select **Edit Role** to switch between `User` and `Admin`. The change takes effect on their next page load.

### Disabling and re-enabling a user

Click the **Disable** action on a user's row to prevent them from logging in without deleting their account. Their content and history are fully preserved. Click **Enable** to restore access.

<Warning>
  You cannot disable or delete your own account — this prevents accidental self-lockout.
</Warning>

### Editing a user's profile

Click a user's name to open their profile panel. You can update their full name, email, phone number, job title, and department.

For users managed by an external directory (LDAP or SCIM), profile fields are read-only in Collabase. Changes must be made in the external system and will be reflected on the next sync.

### Deleting a user

Click the **Delete** action on any user row to permanently remove the account. This cannot be undone. Content created by the user (pages, tasks, comments) is retained — only the account is removed.

### Unlocking a locked account

If brute-force protection is active, accounts lock temporarily after repeated failed login attempts. A **Locked** indicator appears on the user's row. Open the user profile panel and click **Unlock Account** to clear the lock immediately.

## Groups

A group is a named collection of users. Instead of granting Space access to individual users, you grant access to the group — all members inherit that access automatically.

Use groups to manage access for teams or departments. When someone joins or leaves a team, you add or remove them from the group rather than updating every Space individually.

### Creating a group

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Admin → Groups">
    Click **New Group** in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the group">
    Enter a name (required) and an optional description explaining the group's purpose.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create">
    The group appears in the group list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Adding members to a group

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the group">
    Click any group to open its detail panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the Members tab">
    The Members tab shows all current members.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search and add users">
    Type a name or email in the search box and click **Add** next to the user you want.
  </Step>
</Steps>

To remove a member, hover over their row and click the remove button.

### Granting a group access to a Space

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the group and go to the Space Access tab">
    This tab lists all Spaces the group currently has access to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a Space and role">
    Choose the Space and the role the group members will have within it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Save">
    All members of the group can now access the Space with the assigned role.
  </Step>
</Steps>

To revoke access, click the remove button on the Space row in the Space Access tab.

## SCIM v2 provisioning

Collabase implements SCIM 2.0, which lets your identity provider (Okta, Microsoft Entra, JumpCloud, etc.) automatically provision and deprovision users and groups without manual intervention.

**SCIM endpoints:**

| Resource | Endpoint |
|---|---|
| Users | `/api/scim/v2/Users` |
| Groups | `/api/scim/v2/Groups` |
| Service provider config | `/api/scim/v2/ServiceProviderConfig` |
| Schemas | `/api/scim/v2/Schemas` |

All SCIM requests must include a bearer token in the `Authorization` header.

### Generating a SCIM token

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Admin → Users">
    Scroll to the **SCIM Provisioning** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate a new token">
    Click **Generate Token**. The token is displayed once — copy it immediately and store it securely.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your identity provider">
    Paste the token into your IdP's SCIM bearer token field and set the SCIM base URL to `https://your-collabase-domain/api/scim/v2`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  The plaintext token is shown only once. If you lose it, delete the token and generate a new one. Old tokens are immediately invalidated on deletion.
</Warning>

### How SCIM provisioning works

When your identity provider pushes a change to Collabase via SCIM:

- **New users** are created with the `User` global role by default (configurable in your IdP).
- **Updated users** have their profile fields (name, department, job title) synced from the directory.
- **Deprovisioned users** have their accounts disabled in Collabase — their content is not deleted.
- **Groups** are created or updated in Collabase and their memberships are kept in sync.

<Tip>
  Users provisioned via SCIM are shown with a **SCIM** badge in the Directory column of the users table. Their profile fields are read-only in the Collabase UI — all changes must be made in your identity provider.
</Tip>
